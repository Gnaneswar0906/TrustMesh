## 1. Problem Statement

Traditional Raft consensus treats all nodes equally — a malicious node's vote
counts the same as an honest node's vote.  This becomes a critical vulnerability
when a significant fraction of nodes are compromised or faulty.

**TrustMesh** introduces dynamic trust scores that adapt based on each node's
historical behavior.  Votes are weighted by trust, so well-behaved nodes have
more influence while repeatedly incorrect or unresponsive nodes are marginalized.

## 4. Comparison & Analysis

### Key Findings

1. **Scenario 1 (All Good):** Both systems achieve identical accuracy since
   there are no adversaries.  This validates baseline correctness.

2. **Scenario 2 (20% Malicious):** Raft's accuracy begins to degrade because
   malicious nodes contribute equal weight.  TrustMesh quickly reduces the
   trust of misbehaving nodes, maintaining superior accuracy.

3. **Scenario 3 (50% Malicious):** Raft breaks down significantly — in many
   rounds the malicious majority can override honest nodes.  TrustMesh still
   performs well because malicious nodes accumulate negative trust adjustments
   and their votes carry minimal weight.

### Why TrustMesh Is Better

- **Adaptive:** Trust scores evolve over time, isolating bad actors.
- **Weighted voting:** High-trust nodes dominate decisions, reducing adversarial impact.
- **Stable leadership:** Leaders are elected based on trust, not just majority.
